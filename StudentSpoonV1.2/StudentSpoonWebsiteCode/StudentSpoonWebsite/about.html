About the project

Student Spoon is designed around how students actually cook.

It is a prototype community recipe platform that combines affordable meal ideas with simple ingredient searching, budget tips and food-waste reduction.

What problem does it solve?

Students may have a limited food budget, little spare time, limited kitchen equipment and ingredients that need using before they go to waste. Student Spoon gives a faster route to practical recipes than a long advice article and keeps the information needed to choose a meal in one place.

Who is it for?

The main audience is students aged roughly 16-25 who are in further or higher education, may be living independently and want meals that are affordable, simple and realistic to cook.

What the prototype includes

  • Ingredient and recipe-name searching
  • Student-focused recipe filters
  • Clear recipe cards with cost, time and difficulty
  • A recipe submission form with validation
  • Ratings and comments stored locally
  • Budget tips and a cost-per-serving calculator

Accessibility commitment

The website uses a logical heading order, visible keyboard focus, labelled controls, a skip link, responsive layouts and text labels alongside icons. The site should still be tested with real users and browser accessibility tools before it is treated as a finished public website.

Prototype limitations

Prices are illustrative estimates. Recipe submissions, ratings and comments are stored in the browser only and are not moderated or shared publicly. A live version would need secure accounts, a database, content moderation and a privacy policy.
